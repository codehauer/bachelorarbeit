# Rules by advisor

## Language Time

- Introduction: Future
- Main Part: Present
- Conclusions: Past

## General Language Rules

- Rather use "we are doing..." then "I am doing..."
- Try to mostly use passive
- The work can reference itself

## Structural Rules

- Definitions and special words should be explained in the background chapter
- Key research results should be mentioned three times: in the discussions, in the introduction, in the results, and in the abstract (serves the purpose of reducing the mental load on the reader)

## Form Rules

- The title should mention ”Professorship for Applied Machine Learning” (not chair)
- include an abstract
- include a "Eidesstattliche Erklärung"
- include a statement regarding AI
- use consistent capitalization in section headers
- use proper section titles
- do proper grammar and spell checking

## Content Rules

- motivate your work
- state your research goal or research question explicitly and precisely
- provide an explicit (potentially inclusive) answer to your research question
- reflect upon the limitations of your work

## Citation Rules

- properly indicate direct citations with quotes
- Always cite the papers from which you took ideas. If so, for this report indicate the source in each paragraph you use it.
- If possible, cite from scientific publications, not blogs/websites/youtube, etc...
- Did I sufficiently work through related work/literature? Aim for citing at minimum 1 citation per page different papers/books
- Do you have a reference section and cite papers consistently? Do not cite preprints (arxiv) if there is a full publication out already.
